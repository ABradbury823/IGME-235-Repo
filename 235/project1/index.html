Projects

Personal Eyes

Personal Eyes is a 3D survival horror game made in Unity3D that I developed with a team of fellow students from June 2021 until May 2022. The gameplay primarily revolves around the premise of gaining access to a third person camera as a resource rather than a feature. My work on the project involved creating 3D environment models and textures, inspired by PS2-era graphics from titles like Fatal Frame and the earlier Resident Evil games. My workflow included collaborating with the creative lead, concept artist, and other 3D artists to make sure a cohesive artstyle was maintained. As a part of RIT's MAGIC Maker program, I worked full-time developing the game, attending weekly team stand-ups as well as triweekly stand-ups with Rockstar Games to gather feedback from industry professionals. We published a demo that includes our first level to itch.io in May 2022.

Atoll Tale

Atoll Tale is a 3D puzzle-platformer I worked on from August to December 2022 with a team of student developers. The player controls Lula, a pirate who found herself shipwrecked on a series of islands in the middle of the ocean. Equipped with nothing but a trusty shovel, the player must navigate the circular levels and dig up as much treasure as they can find in order to repair their ship. I acted as the team lead for this project as well as the team's artist, making both 2D and 3D assets. As the team lead, I handled the Trello board for task management, scheduled team meetings, and made the final say on creative decisions. My artwork got me involved in character modeling for the player and the enemy, environment assets, texture shaders, and UI elements. Inspired by whimisical Nintendo games such as Captain Toad: Treasure Tracker and The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening, Atoll Tale has a highly- stylized artstyle, providing several unique and fun challenges for a first-time project lead. A demo that includes the first level has been uploaded to itch.io.

Falling Hand

Falling Hand is a web application I developed with a partner from November to December 2022. The game is a simple falling sand simulator with a twist: by using the device's camera, the player can control where particles are dropped in the scene. The application uses the HTML Canvas element for drawing visuals, ml5 PoseNet Machine Learning for tracking hand motions, and Bulma for styling. For this project, I was responsible for the falling sand portion of the game as well as the controls. I used ES6 classes to make a grid that draws and controls a typed array of particles that can be one of three types: sand, water, and stone. Combined with the hotkeys on the mouse, the controls allow for a great deal of customization to the experience, even allowing the user to use their mouse if they don't have a working camera. The website can be found here if you'd like to try it out!

About Me

I am a Gameplay Programmer located in Rochester, New York. I currently attend the Rochester Institute of Technology, majoring in Game Design and Development. When I'm not doing coursework or working on my most recent project, I am frequently practicing my Japanese reading, writing, and speaking ability. My two passions, game-making and Japanese, were formed back in my childhood when I played tons of video games and watched plenty of anime. I was always curious what went into making games and was fascinated by how the hardwork of a team of people culminated in the creation of my favorite games.
